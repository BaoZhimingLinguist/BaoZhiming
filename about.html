Hi, I am a theoretical linguist working in the Department of English Language and Literature at the National University of Singapore (NUS). I joined NUS in 1993.

Research Interests

My current research focuses on two main areas: contact linguistics and phonology.

In contact linguistics, I am interested in how languages acquire new grammatical features from languages they come into contact with, and how social factors influence the development of these features.

In phonology, I am exploring formal methods for analyzing dialect diffusion.

Teaching

I teach modules in English linguistics and contact linguistics at both undergraduate and graduate levels. My area of graduate supervision is broader, including Chinese linguistics and theoretical linguistics.
